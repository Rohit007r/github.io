Why Children?

Millions of children in India still struggle for basic rights—education, nutrition, healthcare, and safety. Their dreams are cut short by poverty, child labour, and child marriage. But YOU have the power to change that.

At CRY – Child Rights and You, we believe every child deserves a happy, healthy, and secure childhood. For over four decades, we have been working tirelessly to ensure that India’s children get the opportunities they deserve. Your generosity can help them build a brighter future.

Who We Are?

Founded in 1979, CRY is India’s leading child rights NGO, dedicated to creating meaningful and lasting change in the lives of underprivileged children. CRY currently operates 144 projects in 20 states and 118 districts.

With your support, we address children’s critical needs by working with parents, teachers, Anganwadi workers, communities, district and state-level governments as well as the children themselves. We work on changing behaviours and practices at the grassroots level and influencing public policy at a systemic level – thereby creating an ecosystem where children are made the nation’s priority. Our key focus areas include

Education

We conduct awareness drives to highlight the importance of education and ensure that all children are enrolled in school.

Protection

We prevent child marriage and child labour by creating awareness about the harmful effects of these practices. Additionally, we connect families with MNREGA job opportunities.

Health and Nutrition

We ensure children receive timely vaccinations and sufficient nutrition by connecting them to government healthcare services

Our Impact in 2024-25:

With the support of donors like YOU, we have transformed the lives of millions:

  • 22,57,372 children impacted overall
  • 2,29,045 children enrolled in school
  • 1,05,940 children empowered through children’s collectives
  • 64,224 children benefitted from digital learning through CRY Support Centres
  • 32,472 children protected from child labour/ marriage/ trafficking
  • 8,292 kitchen gardens set up across 12 states to combat malnutrition

Yet, out of 472 million children in India, millions still need urgent support. Your donation today can give a child a real chance at a better future.

    Our Stories

    Meet Malini - a doctor in the making!

    Malini, from a remote village in Tamil Nadu, overcame hurdles to continue her education and crack NEET. Now, at Tirunelveli Medical College, she’s set to be the first woman doctor in her village.

    Our soccer star - Mamta Gujjar!

    Mamta Gujjar fought against all odds to continue her education and become a footballer! Inspired by her story of struggle, Google selected her for its #SearchForChange campaign.

    How did Rohit become a Military Police Cadet?

    Forced to drop out of school and work as a child labourer at once, Rohit re-enrolled in school and continued his education. Today, he is a part of the Bihar Military Police Academy and hopes to make his nation proud!
